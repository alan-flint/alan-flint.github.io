Project Portfolio

Below is a selection of my personal projects and class projects from the M.S. in Data Science program at the University of San Francisco.

Indigo Music Studio

Generate new music through machine learning with the web application we built using Google's Magenta platform. My responsibilities included Google Analytics tracking, unit testing, and documenting. Source Code

Modeled Earthquake Damage

Predicted damage grade to buildings affected by the 2015 Nepalese earthquake using a RandomForest with features heavily engineered from geographic information. Placed top 4% in the DrivenData.org competition.

Feature Importance

Implemented, explored, and explained different feature importance strategies inlcuding Spearman's Rank, drop column, and permutation importance. Measured their statistical significance with empirical p-value simulations.



Reddit NFL Draft Comments

Sentiment analysis of fans' Reddit comments from the 2020 NFL draft using the VADER Sentiment library. Also trained word embeddings for named entities using a Continous-Bag-Of-Words model in PyTorch

Other Projects

  • Implemented RandomForest, Regularized Regression, and Gradient Boosting machine learning algorithms from scratch in Python
  • Predicted mean artierial pressure and heart rate of critically ill patients with an ensemble of tree based models with 0.92 R-squared (Placed 4th in a class Kaggle competition)
  • Analyzed 7 GB of domestic flight data with Spark on AWS EMR; modeled flight delays with SparkML linear regression and visualized the data with Plotly
  • Built a search engine for a corpus of BBC articles with my own hashtable implementation

k-means Clustering

Coded my own implementation of k-means and k-means++ clustering algorithms in Python. Then compressed images by clustering their RGB values and re-assigning each pixel to the value of it's centroid.

About Me

I recently graduated from the M.S. in Data Science program at the University of San Francisco and completed an internship at Trulia. My passions in the field include modern machine learning and deep learning techniques, experimental design (A/B testing and Multi-Armed bandits), and statistical modeling.

Born in Seattle, Washington, I spent the early years of my life in the Pacific Northwest. I then moved to Maryland where I completed my Bachelor's degree in mathematics and economics from the University of Maryland, College Park. Go Terps! During my undergrad, I spent one year down under, living and studying in the beautiful Sydney, Australia. It was there I discovered how much I enjoyed computational statistics and decided to pursue a career in data science. That persuit led me here, to San Francisco, California. Outside the world of numbers, I enjoy all kinds of sports, visiting National Parks, and downhill skiing.
